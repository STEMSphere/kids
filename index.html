The surprising research on why kids learn better with peers

The Secret Ingredient: Peer Learning

Private 1-on-1 tutoring sounds like the gold standard. But research shows something surprising: Kids learn faster and retain more in small groups of 2-8 students.

Why? Because learning isn't just about absorbing information‚Äîit's about discussion, collaboration, explaining concepts to peers, and seeing problems solved different ways.

  • Hearing another child explain a concept often clicks better than hearing an adult
  • Healthy competition motivates kids to push themselves further
  • Collaboration teaches teamwork‚Äîa skill they'll need their entire life
  • Kids build friendships with peers who share their interests

Our approach: Small enough for personalized attention (2 or 8 students max), large enough for dynamic peer learning. The perfect balance that traditional schools and private tutoring both miss.
